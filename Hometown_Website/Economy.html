About

The economy surrounding Loon Lake is mostly built on farming. Most of the farms are either family run, or they hire one or two people from the village to be farmhands. Outside of that however there is very little economy in the village. The only options people really have for jobs is to be a teacher or a police officer. A majority of the people in the village are either retired or living off welfare and child support. In the summer the economy gets a bit of a boost from people coming to stay at the campgrounds, or they come to golf at the small 9-hole course just outside the village. There is also a place called the Dairy Bar that opens up at the start of the summer. These things provide summer jobs for the young people to make a little bit of money.
